1. Intro

Space Skirmish is a fast-paced 4X card game that focuses on the best elements of my favorite pamphlet games growing up in the 80s.  There’s resource allocation, trading, alliances, back-stabbing, and of course glorious space battles.


2. Setup

In the box you will find: Faction Mats, Space Maps, Ship Cards, Research Cards, Resource Cards, Resource Cubes, Space Maps, dice and pens.


Faction Matts:  Each player grabs a Faction Mat.  This will have your Tech Tree, fleet list, alliances, etc.  Use a pen to customize it with a name for your faction.  Keep the pen, you’ll be using it a bunch.


Research Cards.  Everyone starts with blueprints for colony ships and freighters.  Plus three random research cards.  Shuffle the rest of put them within reach of all the players.


Space Maps.  Everyone gets one space map (3x3 standard sheet card sleeve).  This is where you will place your planets, upgrades, etc.

Play follows a number of mini-games each turn.

3.1.1. Peacetime

Each turn start in peacetime.  Here economies flourish, trade happens and all the factions generally get along.

Each round of peacetime begins with administradium.

4. Shipping Lanes

Shipping lanes exist between the planets and that’s where the freighter live.  

5. Resource Cubes

Three different types of resource cubes.  Resource cubes move form planet to plant along shipping lanes and can get converted to resource cards on proper planets.


5.1. Grey Ore Cubes

Ore resource cubes are produced on or planets and are the raw materiel for building things like ships, research stations, etc. 

5.2. Tan Colonist Cubes

Colonists are the people resource of your realm.  You need them to do things like research technology and fly the ships.

5.3. Green Good Cubes

Market planets produce green goods cubes that get converted to green goods cards at market planets

6.1. Ships

Ships are build at shipyards using blueprints and help you get about in the galaxy.  Ship cards are placed in standard card protectors so you can write on them with dry-erase pens.  Mark up things like what weapons are charged, your speed, and any damage taken.


Your engines generate power on your ship that helps you move and fire weapons. Phasers take 1 unit of power to charge and corps take 1 unit on two consecutive turns.  Once charged, your weapons remain charged until you use them.  You never have enough power to move fast and charge your weapons. 


Speed is Life.  When resolving damage, the faster ship fires first and damage is resolved in order.  


At the end of a skirmish, the winner is the one who did the most damage.


Both parties keep the damage on their ships and return them to the shipyard or homeworld for repairs.  You can only repair ships at a dock.  Your homeworld has 1 doc and your shipyard has 4.



 



6.1.1. Freighters

Freighters can transport 3 cubes from planet to planet and have no weapons.  It’a a good idea to assign escorts to your freighters or have escorts hanging out in shipping lanes.  Place the 3 cubes on the ship card and move the ship card from  space to space on your map. 


Ship Boxes:

  • 4 power form the 4 engines
  • 1 command center
  • 3 hull
  • 3 freight (hit on any weapon hit)




6.1.2. Escorts

Once you research Phasers you can build escorts to defend your freighters or participate in raiding skirmishes.


Ship Boxes:

  • 4 power form the 4 engines
  • 2 Phasers
  • 1 command center
  • 3 hull
